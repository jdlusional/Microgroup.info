Almanac

Almanac finds the funders and open grants that fit a nonprofit, ranks them by fit, and shows the public source behind every one. This page runs Almanac against a made-up organization so you can see the output before you commission your own.

How to read this page. Second Shift Learning Collaborative does not exist. It is an illustrative adult-literacy nonprofit invented for this demo, and every number attached to it was invented along with it. No real client's data appears anywhere on this page.

The funders and grants are real. Each one is an actual foundation or program that funds adult literacy and workforce readiness, and each carries a link to its own page. Amounts, deadlines, and eligibility rules are quoted from those pages, or marked as unconfirmed where the funder does not publish them. Nothing about a real funder is estimated or invented here. What is illustrative is the match itself: the judgment that these funders suit this particular (made-up) organization.

Each entry below compiles all three pieces for one organization — the ranked match rationale, the funder's own IRS filing data, and a currently open grant opportunity or letter-of-inquiry channel — instead of three separate lists a reader has to cross-reference by hand. An organization is only included here once all three are available and its opportunity is actionable today: an open application window or a live inquiry channel, not a closed cycle. A funder that clears the match and the IRS profile but has no current opening does not appear, the same way a funder with an open cycle but no traceable IRS filing would not appear either.

Method

How this was built, and what it cannot tell you

Where this comes from. Every funder and every grant on this page is drawn from that funder's own public record: its IRS Form 990 filings, its published guidelines, and its own grant pages. Each match carries a link back to the source it came from, so nothing here has to be taken on trust. Where a funder does not publish a figure — an award range, a deadline, an eligibility rule — that gap is marked as unconfirmed rather than filled in.

What Almanac will not do. It does not invent a fact about a funder. An unpublished deadline is reported as unpublished, not replaced with a plausible-looking date, because a made-up deadline is worse than a missing one: a missing deadline gets checked, and an invented one gets relied on. It also keeps a hard line between a funder that has actually given to this organization before and a funder that merely looks like a good fit. A candidate is never dressed up as a confirmed prior funder.

What it cannot tell you. Form 990 filings reach the public a year or more after the fact, so a funder's priorities may already have moved. A foundation that funds this kind of work is not therefore open to you: many accept no unsolicited proposals at all, and some of the best matches here will be closed doors for reasons no filing records. The ranking is a judgment about fit, not a prediction of success. Treat every row as a lead to verify against the funder's own page before you spend real time on it.
