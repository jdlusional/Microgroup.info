Concordance

The people who govern the foundations that already fund work like yours, and who on your board can reach them.

Almanac finds the institutions. Concordance finds the humans inside them. It reads the officers, directors and trustees a private foundation lists on its own Form 990-PF, sets them against what that foundation actually granted, and shows the grants that justify every match. It does not estimate anyone's wealth.

What this page is

This is a demonstration. The subject organization, Second Shift Learning Collaborative, is invented, so that the page can be shown to anyone without exposing a client's research. The foundations, the trustees, the grants and the dollar amounts below are all real, and all come from public IRS filings.

Board data is as last filed, not as it stands today. Every person named here comes from a Form 990-PF for tax year 2023, and those filings reach the public 12 to 24 months after the fact. Boards turn over in that window. While this tool was being built, its first real query surfaced a director who had already left the board she was still listed on. The filing was not wrong; it was old. Confirm anyone is still serving before you act on their name.
Why there are no introduction paths on this page. Concordance's second half maps who on your board can reach a foundation's board, either by sitting on both or by sharing a third board with a trustee. That needs a real board to start from, and it needs the organization to sit inside the board-interlock graph. This demo organization is fictional and has no board, so no paths are shown. Nothing has been invented to fill the gap. For a real client inside the graph, this is the part of the tool no commercial competitor can reproduce.

Method

How this was built, and what it cannot tell you

Where this comes from. Trustees come from the repeatable officer, director and trustee block that every private foundation files on its Form 990-PF. The IRS instructs filers not to put home addresses in that block, so the dataset carries none. Grants come from the same filings, itemized by recipient, amount and stated purpose. A foundation appears here only if it has both, which is why the universe is the size it is and no larger.

What Concordance will not do. It does not estimate wealth, and it never will. There is no capacity score, no net-worth model, and no political-giving data anywhere in it. Commercial prospect-research tools are built on licensed wealth data that is not public, and a wealth guess dressed up as a number is worse than no number at all. This tool ranks affinity and access, which are matters of public record, and it stops there.

What it cannot tell you. Names are matched as strings, so a person who appears under two spellings may be missed, and two different people who share a name may be conflated; low-confidence matches are labelled rather than asserted. The filings are a year or more old, and a board can turn over in that time. A foundation that funds your cause is not therefore open to you, and many private foundations accept no unsolicited proposals at all. Treat every row as a lead to verify, not a conclusion.
