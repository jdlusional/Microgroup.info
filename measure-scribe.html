Method

How this was built, and what it cannot tell you

Where this comes from. Each draft is built from two sources and no others: the Almanac match that identified the funder, with that funder's own published requirements, and the organization's own public record: its website, its filings, and the materials it has already put into the world. Every substantive claim in a draft traces back to one of those two places.

What Scribe will not do. It does not invent facts about the organization. When an application asks for something only the applicant holds (a budget line, an outcome figure, a board resolution, a letter of support), Scribe does not guess at it. It leaves the space empty and puts the item on the list of what is still needed. This is the whole discipline of the tool: an invented number is worse than a blank one, because a blank gets filled in before it is filed and an invention gets submitted.

What it cannot tell you. A draft is a draft, not a submission. Funder requirements change, portals impose their own formats and character limits, and the published guidance a draft was built from can already be out of date. Everything here has to be checked against the funder's live application before it is filed, and the organization's own people are the only ones who can confirm that what it says about them is true today. Scribe is built to remove the blank page. It is not built to remove the review.
