Measure · Funding Priorities Worksheet

Help us find funding that actually fits you.

Everything we've matched you to so far (funders, open grants, program alignment) has been built from public filings alone. We've never actually asked you what you need. This worksheet is that ask: ten questions, grouped into five areas, about your budget, your preferences, and where you stand right now. They're all required, but most take just a sentence or two, and your answers are what make the matching real.

MONEY

What you're working with

Q1What's your current annual operating budget, and roughly how does it split across programs?

Ballpark is fine. Per-program figures help even more, if you track them that way.

Q2Do you need general operating support, program-restricted funding, or both, and are you open to multi-year commitments?
Q3Are there funder types, industries, or specific funders you'd want us to avoid or deprioritize?

Sources that conflict with your mission, or anywhere you'd rather not be listed as a grantee.

TIMING

Where things stand right now

Q4Any near-term cash gaps, fiscal year-end deadlines, or opportunities you're already pursuing?

The last part matters most: tell us what's already in motion so we don't duplicate it.

Q5Beyond what's in public filings, what's the real state of your funder relationships?

Which past funders are warm and worth re-approaching, which are dormant, and any where there's friction we should know about before we suggest them again.

CAPACITY

What you can actually take on

Q6Of your four programs, which most needs funding right now?
Q7How much grant-writing bandwidth do you actually have right now?

Be honest: this shapes how many opportunities are worth surfacing versus how few, well-matched ones.

POSITIONING

How you see yourselves

Q8Your IRS filing lists you under Education (NTEE B05). Is that still how you'd describe the core of the work?

Our read is that "data justice / racial equity" fits better than "education", but you'd know.

Q9Do Houston and Dallas have real, active program presence, or are they mostly aspirational at this point?

Matters because some funders require verifiable local presence, not just a stated service area.

WISHLIST

What you wish existed

Q10If this funder-matching tool could do anything for you, what would make it genuinely indispensable?

Think aspirationally: features, reports, alerts, integrations. Blue-sky is welcome; this shapes what we build next.
