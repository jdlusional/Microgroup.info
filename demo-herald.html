Herald

Almanac finds your funders. Herald tells you the day one of them opens a door: a cycle opens, a deadline posts, guidelines change, or a page quietly dies.

Herald re-fetches every funder page behind an Almanac watch-list on a daily cadence, compares each one against its stored snapshot, and reports only what it can show. Herald never interprets a change it cannot show you. Every alert carries the page, the fetch time, and the actual before/after text. A page it could not reach is reported as “couldn't check”: silence is never treated as “no change.”

What an alert looks like

These are format specimens, not detections. No funder below has actually changed anything; the text illustrates the four alert classes Herald can raise, in the shape a real one would arrive.

cycle opened
[Specimen] A watch-list funder's page now shows application language (“now accepting applications”) where the stored snapshot said the cycle was closed. The alert carries the added lines and a link to the page.
deadline
[Specimen] A new date appears in the changed region that differs from the deadline Almanac recorded. The alert shows the old and new text side by side; Herald does not decide which date governs: you read the diff.
guidelines changed
[Specimen] Material text changed that is neither a date nor an open/close flip: eligibility wording, award size, focus areas. The alert attaches the diff excerpt for review before anyone redrafts an application.
page dead
[Specimen] A monitored page returns a true 404 after retry (challenges and blocks are excluded by design). The alert notes the last good fetch, so the trail back to the funder's current site starts from something real.
Method

How this works, and what it cannot tell you

Where this comes from. The watch-list is the Almanac build's own output: the funder and grant pages it cited as sources. Herald fetches each page daily with an ordinary browser identity, reduces it to its visible text, and compares that against the snapshot from the previous fetch. Cosmetic churn (copyright years, cookie banners, navigation) is filtered before anything is classified.

What Herald will not do. It never reports a change it cannot show: every alert carries the diff itself. It never treats an unreachable page as an unchanged one, and never treats a bot-protection challenge as a dead grant. And it sends nothing when nothing happened: a monthly “all quiet, every page checked” note is the proof of life, not a stream of manufactured urgency.

What it cannot tell you. Some funder pages render entirely in the browser and expose no text to a simple fetch; Herald marks those “manual check only” rather than pretending to watch them. A changed page still needs a human read: Herald finds the change, it does not interpret the funder's intent. And a page that never mentions its next cycle cannot be monitored into mentioning it.
