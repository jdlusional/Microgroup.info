Atlas

Atlas benchmarks a corporate foundation's giving against real aggregate statistics computed from actual IRS Form 990-PF Part XV filings by Texas-registered foundations — not a claim about the entire national universe of corporate foundations. This page runs Atlas against a made-up foundation so you can see the output before you commission your own.

How to read this page. Thornbury Materials Foundation does not exist. It is an invented corporate foundation, made up for this demo along with every figure attached to it — founding date, assets, grant volume, recipient list. No real client's data appears anywhere on this page.

The benchmark statistics are real: aggregate percentile bands, recipient-state distribution, and purpose-string frequency, computed directly from 353,820 real Part XV grant rows filed by 8,754 distinct Texas-registered 990-PF foundations. Nothing about the benchmark is invented. What is illustrative is Thornbury itself — a stand-in so you can see how a real foundation's figures would sit against this real peer set.

Method

How this was built, and what it cannot tell you

Where this comes from. The peer-benchmark figures on this page are computed from tx_990pf_grants_paid.csv, 278,608 real grant rows drawn from Part XV of Form 990-PF filings by Texas-registered private foundations. core_2024_990pf_merged.csv, a separate stratified sample carrying asset and payout-ratio fields, exists in this pipeline but is not joined into this demo — see the callout above.

What Atlas will not do. It will not estimate or infer a payout ratio, an asset figure, or any core-file-dependent metric for a real company until a working resolution join is built and verified. It will not present this Texas-filer peer pool as a national corporate-foundation universe — every benchmark number on this page is scoped explicitly to Texas-registered filers.

What it cannot tell you. It cannot currently benchmark payout ratio or asset-based metrics at all. It cannot categorize program areas beyond raw purpose-string text, because the source field is uncoded free text with no controlled vocabulary — an abbreviation like "MEF" appears exactly as filed, undecoded. Year-over-year growth is only available for filers with more than one tax_year present in the corpus.
