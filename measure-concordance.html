Concordance

The people who govern the foundations that already fund work like Measure's, and whether anyone on the board can reach them.

Prepared for Measure (wemeasure.org). Almanac finds the institutions. Concordance finds the humans inside them. It reads the officers, directors and trustees each private foundation lists on its own Form 990-PF, sets them against what that foundation actually granted, and shows the grants that justify every match. It does not estimate anyone's wealth.

What this page is

Every foundation, trustee, grant and dollar amount below is real, and all of it comes from public IRS filings. This page was prepared for Measure and is shared with Measure directly; it is not listed or indexed anywhere.

Board data is as last filed, not as it stands today. Every person named here comes from a Form 990-PF for tax year 2023, and those filings reach the public 12 to 24 months after the fact. Boards turn over in that window. Confirm anyone is still serving before you act on their name. The same caveat applies to Measure's own board roster below, which comes from filings, not from the organization's current records.
The access half

Introduction paths

A direct path is a person who sits on Measure's board and a foundation's board at once. A shared path is a Measure board member who sits on some third board alongside a foundation trustee. Names are matched conservatively (exact match after normalization, no fuzzy guessing), and every path carries a confidence label; a short, common name shared across several boards is labelled rather than asserted.

Method

How this was built, and what it cannot tell you

Where this comes from. Trustees come from the repeatable officer, director and trustee block that every private foundation files on its Form 990-PF. The IRS instructs filers not to put home addresses in that block, so the dataset carries none. Grants come from the same filings, itemized by recipient, amount and stated purpose. A foundation appears here only if it has both, which is why the universe is the size it is and no larger.

How the introduction paths were checked. Measure's own directors, as listed on filings, were matched against every trustee in the universe and against an Austin-scoped board-interlock graph built from the same public filings. Matching is deliberately conservative: exact match on a normalized name, no nickname or initial expansion. That means a person filed under two spellings can be missed: the check under-finds rather than invents. A result of zero paths is therefore a real finding about the filed record, not proof that no relationship exists in the world.

What Concordance will not do. It does not estimate wealth, and it never will. There is no capacity score, no net-worth model, and no political-giving data anywhere in it. Commercial prospect-research tools are built on licensed wealth data that is not public, and a wealth guess dressed up as a number is worse than no number at all. This tool ranks affinity and access, which are matters of public record, and it stops there.

What it cannot tell you. The filings are a year or more old, and a board can turn over in that time. A foundation that funds your cause is not therefore open to you, and many private foundations accept no unsolicited proposals at all. Treat every row as a lead to verify, not a conclusion.
